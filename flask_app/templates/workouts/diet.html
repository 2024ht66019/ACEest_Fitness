Nutritional Diet Guide

Nutrition Fundamentals

Proper nutrition is essential for achieving your fitness goals. Choose a plan that aligns with your objectives and remember that consistency is key to success.

Weight Loss Focus (Calorie Deficit)

Create a caloric deficit while maintaining nutrition

Breakfast
  • Oatmeal with berries (high fiber)
  • Greek yogurt with chia seeds
  • Green smoothie with spinach
  • Whole grain toast with avocado
Lunch
  • Grilled chicken/tofu salad
  • Quinoa bowl with vegetables
  • Lentil soup with whole grain bread
  • Turkey wrap with lettuce
Dinner
  • Vegetable soup with lentils
  • Baked fish with steamed vegetables
  • Stir-fried tofu with broccoli
  • Grilled chicken breast with salad
Key Principle: Consume 300-500 calories less than your TDEE. Focus on high-volume, low-calorie foods to stay satisfied while maintaining a deficit.

Muscle Gain Focus (High Protein)

Increase protein intake and create a caloric surplus

Breakfast
  • 3-egg omelet with vegetables
  • Protein pancakes with peanut butter
  • Whole wheat toast with eggs
  • Protein shake with banana
Lunch
  • Chicken breast with quinoa
  • Salmon with sweet potato
  • Lean beef with brown rice
  • Turkey meatballs with pasta
Dinner
  • Grilled steak with vegetables
  • Chicken stir-fry with rice
  • Tuna with whole grain pasta
  • Tofu scramble with beans
Key Principle: Consume 250-500 calories more than your TDEE. Aim for 1.6-2.2g of protein per kg of body weight. Don't forget post-workout protein within 30 minutes!

Endurance Focus (Complex Carbs)

Fuel your body with sustainable energy sources

Pre-Workout
  • Banana with peanut butter
  • Oatmeal with honey
  • Whole grain toast with jam
  • Energy bars (natural)
Main Meals
  • Whole grain pasta with light sauce
  • Brown rice with grilled chicken
  • Sweet potato with salmon
  • Quinoa bowl with vegetables
Recovery
  • Chocolate milk
  • Protein shake with fruit
  • Greek yogurt with granola
  • Whole grain sandwich
Key Principle: Carbohydrates should make up 55-65% of your diet. Focus on complex carbs for sustained energy and quick carbs for immediate pre/post-workout fuel.
